System Overview

Project Summary

This document summarizes the agreed design goals for the electronics and electrical system for Randy Dersham's 22-foot Calkins Bartender restoration.

Primary Mission

Build a dependable electronics package optimized for the Oregon coast while remaining equally capable on inland lakes such as Odell Lake.

Design Philosophy

Keep the helm clean and traditional. Retain analog engine instruments for redundancy while adding modern digital capabilities through a Garmin chartplotter and NMEA 2000 network. Freeze major design decisions once approved and build future work from that baseline.

Electrical Architecture

Separate Engine, Navigation, and House power buses. Use marine-grade wiring, quality circuit protection, and reserve capacity for future radar, heading sensor, and autopilot.

Engine Monitoring Goals

Display RPM, oil pressure, coolant temperature, battery voltage, fuel level, fuel flow, fuel used, and range on the Garmin. Install independent low-oil-pressure and high-temperature alarms.

Frozen Design Decisions

  • Garmin GPSMAP 1042xsv chartplotter
  • Airmar B175M 0° tilted-element through-hull transducer
  • ICOM M510BB black-box VHF with CommandMic
  • Dedicated Class B AIS transceiver
  • NMEA 2000 backbone
  • Analog tachometer, oil pressure, water temperature, voltmeter and fuel gauge retained
  • Digital engine monitoring added while preserving analog gauges

Airmar B175M Installation

Installation Notes

  1. Install in a solid laminate area near the keel line. Avoid areas with air bubbles, water flow turbulence, or directly behind hull steps.
  2. Ensure the path inside the hull is clear of any stringers, bulkheads, or electrical wiring.
  3. Apply a generous bead of high-quality marine sealant (e.g., 3M 4200 or 5200) to both the transducer flange and the hull opening.
  4. Do not over-tighten the bronze locking nut, as this can damage the mahogany hull structure or strip the threads.
  5. Torque the lock nut to 25–30 ft-lbs using a custom spanner wrench or large adjustable wrench.
  6. Verify correct transducer tilt angle (e.g., 0°, 12°, or 20° version matches deadrise) before tightening.
